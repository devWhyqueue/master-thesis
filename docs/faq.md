# FAQ

## What is the difference between digital pathology and computational pathology?

**Digital pathology** refers to the foundational infrastructure and tools used to digitize glass pathology slides into whole-slide images, as well as the systems required for their storage, management, and review. 

**Computational pathology (CPath)** builds upon this digital foundation. It refers to the use of computational methods—specifically artificial intelligence and machine learning—to actively analyze those digitized images. It is often described as a "big-data" approach that combines digital pathology images with patient metadata to extract complex patterns, identify hidden features, and study diseases. 

In short, digital pathology is the *digitization and management* of pathology data, while computational pathology is the *algorithmic analysis* of that data.
